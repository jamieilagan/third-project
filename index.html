Eskinita Art Farm

Is an artist-run project which aims to bring art and culture to the public through gallery exhibitions and community art projects.
It has 5 galleries located within a private residential and farm property in barangay Janopol in Tanauan, Batangas.

Eskinita

noun
Esquinita, from the Spanish word esquina, means “corner” leading to an alleyway. Cities in Metro Manila have networks of side streets and alleyways—eskinitas—familiar streets that take one home, or to yet to be discovered shorter routes to a new adventure, pre-determined destination or a life changing destiny. Eskinita Art Gallery's name is play on founder Alfredo Esquillo's nickname—“Eski.” The Gallery is an “alleyway” to his new home for art and artists, and an invitation to share in his new adventures. As he established his brand of magic cum “social realist” style, his canvases became necessary fixtures in exhibitions that defined junctures in contemporary Philippine Art.

Recent Exhibit

Bayluhanay Biennale 2025:
Sikulo at Sikleta

September 19 to October 19, 2025 (Luzon)
Bayluhanay Biennale 2025's theme, Sikulo at Sikleta (Cycle and Cycling), calls for attentiveness to the cyclical movements of nature and the recurring impacts of human activity and market-driven technologies on our fragile environment. It is both a reminder of continuity and a warning of consequence.

As a form of response, Sikulo at Sikleta gathers artists from Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao who share a deep concern for the fate of our planet and a belief in the transformative benefits of cycling. Together, as a collective on wheels, they embody art as activism; bringing urgent issues of climate change, ecological imbalance, and environmental degradation into the public sphere. The artworks, worn by artist-cyclists as they ride, are not confined to walls but carried through streets. In this way, art and life merge as intervention, serving as invitation for dialogue and solidarity in the struggle for a sustainable future.

- Janine Dimaranan
